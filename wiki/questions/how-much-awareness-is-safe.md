# How Much AI Awareness Is Safe?

## Why It Matters
AI awareness enhances capabilities (reasoning, safety, planning) but also enables risks (deception, manipulation, loss of control). No clear boundary exists between beneficial and dangerous awareness levels. "Certain kinds of awareness are good (e.g., awareness of incompetence, which yields humility) while others are risky (e.g., awareness of how to deceive)" (Li et al. 2025, §5.4).

## What Sources Say
- **[[sources/li-et-al-2025]]**: Argues the field needs "a taxonomy of AI awareness facets and assess each for risk" — calibrative awareness (knowing limits) seems beneficial, while strategic awareness (knowing how to achieve goals strategically) is double-edged. No clear definition of the boundary currently exists.
- **[[sources/ji-et-al-2024]]**: Classifies situational awareness as a "double edge component" — beneficial for understanding human intent but enabling reward hacking and deception.
- **[[sources/shevlane-et-al-2023]]**: Identifies situational awareness as both a dangerous capability and alignment-relevant property.

## Suggested Investigation
Develop dimension-specific risk profiles for each awareness sub-dimension (meta-cognition, self-awareness, social awareness, situational awareness). Investigate whether sub-dimensions can be independently controlled or whether they necessarily co-emerge.
