# Can AI Risk Concepts Be Unified Into a Shared Ontology?

## Why It Matters

The AI risk field lacks consistent shared definitions for core concepts. The same term (e.g., "power-seeking") is classified as a behavior, instrumental goal, tendency, drive, or socioeconomic risk depending on the source. Without consistent definitions, cross-study comparison, cumulative knowledge building, and effective policy development are impeded.

## What Sources Say

- [[sources/slattery-et-al-2024]]: Found "overlapping clusters of risk concepts and categories" across 65 documents, but "the specificity, consistency, and coherence of the definitions of these concepts could be significantly improved." Calls for development of "an ontology" with "component terms with consistent and shared definitions" (citing Marques et al. 2024). Notes that the EU-US Trade and Technology Council has stated "shared terminologies and taxonomies are essential for operationalizing trustworthy AI and risk management."
- [[sources/fourie-2025]]: Proposes an Aristotelian ontological framework for instrumental goals, arguing for structural (rather than eliminative) treatment of AI safety concepts.

## Suggested Investigation

- Compare classification vocabularies across all wiki sources systematically
- Map where the same concept receives different level-of-analysis classifications
- Identify which disagreements are genuine (reflecting different theoretical commitments) vs. merely terminological
- Develop proposed canonical definitions for core concepts that accommodate multiple valid levels of analysis
