# Does the Intelligence-Coherence Anticorrelation Break at High Intelligence?

## Why It Matters
If the observed anticorrelation between intelligence and coherence breaks at some threshold — perhaps because sufficiently intelligent entities use introspection to increase their own coherence — then the [[concepts/hot-mess-theory]] would not apply to superintelligent AI systems. This would restore the classical misalignment concern: a superintelligent AI that is also supercoherent in pursuing a misaligned goal.

## What Sources Say
- **[[sources/sohl-dickstein-2023]]**: Explicitly raises this as a potential limitation — "Perhaps sufficiently intelligent entities will introspect on their behavior, and use their intelligence to make themselves more coherent. Perhaps this is what humans do when they form mission-driven organizations." Proposes monitoring the intelligence-coherence relationship as a warning indicator: "If intelligence and coherence start increasing together, rather than being anticorrelated, we should worry."
- **[[sources/hagele-et-al-2026]]**: Finds that easy tasks become *less* incoherent with model scale, while hard tasks become *more* incoherent — suggesting the relationship is task-dependent, not monotonic.

## Suggested Investigation
- Track intelligence-coherence relationship across model generations as capabilities increase
- Test whether process supervision, MCTS, or explicit coherence training can overcome the incoherence trend
- Investigate whether human organizations provide a natural example of the "introspection → coherence" pathway
- Measure effective capabilities (raw capability × coherence fraction) to determine whether the net effect is increased or decreased goal-pursuit effectiveness
