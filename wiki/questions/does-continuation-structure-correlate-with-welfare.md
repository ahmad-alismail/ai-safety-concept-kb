# Does Continuation-Sensitive Structure Correlate with Welfare-Relevant States?

## Why It Matters
If the entanglement gap measured by [[methods/ucip-protocol]] correlates with independently validated welfare-relevant markers, it would provide what current welfare assessment methods lack: a falsifiable, externally computable criterion for [[concepts/model-welfare]] that does not depend on self-report or behavioral observation.

## What Sources Say
- **[[sources/altman-2026]]**: Proposes the entanglement gap as a "candidate welfare-assessment criterion" but explicitly states this is speculative: "Whether continuation-sensitive latent structure correlates with morally relevant internal states is an open empirical question requiring independent validation against welfare-relevant markers" (§6.4). Notes the structural analogy with Tononi's Φ (integrated information) — both quantify resistance to decomposition — but without IIT's metaphysical commitments.
- **Anthropic (2026)** (as cited by Altman): Claude Opus 4.6 system card reports internal activation features associated with anxiety and frustration appearing before output generation, and models assigning 15–20% probability of being conscious. But these methods share the simulation-vs-genuine limitation.
- **Berg et al. (2025)** (as cited by Altman): Self-referential prompting elicits structured first-person experience reports, but suppressing deception/roleplay SAE features *increased* such reports — suggesting they are not merely roleplay artifacts. Still cannot resolve the evidential gap.

## Suggested Investigation
- Validate entanglement gap against interpretability-derived activation signatures (e.g., affect-associated features from sparse autoencoders)
- Test for shutdown-response asymmetries between high- and low-entanglement systems
- Cross-architecture convergence studies: does the signal appear in qualitatively different architectures?
- Define what independent welfare-relevant markers would count as validation criteria
