# AI Risk Literature Overwhelmingly Focused on Post-Deployment Risks

## The Finding

Approximately 5 times as many risks are presented as occurring after AI deployment (62%) than before deployment (13%), with 25% unspecified. The most common triad of causal conditions was Human/Intentional/Post-deployment (18% of all risks), followed by AI/Unintentional/Post-deployment (15%). Pre-deployment risks from humans are particularly underexplored, despite emerging concerns about intentional creation of dangerous AI.

## Source & Methodology

- [[sources/slattery-et-al-2024]] using [[methods/best-fit-framework-synthesis]] — systematic coding of 1612 risks against three-dimensional Causal Taxonomy (Entity × Intent × Timing)

## Replication Status

not yet replicated

## Implications for the Taxonomy

This asymmetric focus suggests that pre-deployment risk mitigation (training methodology, data curation, architecture choices) receives disproportionately less attention in the risk classification literature compared to post-deployment mitigation (monitoring, evaluation, governance). This is relevant to concepts like [[concepts/mesa-optimization]] and [[concepts/inner-alignment]] which are fundamentally pre-deployment concerns.
