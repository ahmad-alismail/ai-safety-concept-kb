# Paraphrase Augmentation Is Necessary and Sufficient for SOC Reasoning

## The Finding
Data augmentation via paraphrasing is both necessary and sufficient to enable 1-hop out-of-context reasoning in finetuned LLMs. Without augmentation (Experiment 1a), models score at baseline (~2%). With 300 paraphrased descriptions per chatbot (Experiment 1b), GPT-3-175B scores ~17% even without auxiliary demonstrations. Demonstrations alone (without paraphrasing) do not improve performance. As little as 10% augmentation (mixed with repeated descriptions) significantly outperforms baseline.

Related finding: models trained on "a=b" fail to infer "b=a" (the Curse of Reversal), suggesting augmentation that shuffles item order is important.

## Source & Methodology
- [[sources/berglund-et-al-2023]] using [[methods/out-of-context-chatbots]]
- Paraphrases generated by ChatGPT, varying style and phrasing while preserving semantic content
- Ablation: vary fraction of paraphrased vs. repeated descriptions while holding dataset size fixed

## Replication Status
Not yet independently replicated.

## Implications for the Taxonomy
Suggests that [[concepts/out-of-context-reasoning]] depends not just on memorization but on forming robust, generalizable representations of declarative facts. The diversity of phrasings in real pretraining data may naturally provide this augmentation, meaning SOC reasoning could be stronger from pretraining than these finetuning experiments suggest.
