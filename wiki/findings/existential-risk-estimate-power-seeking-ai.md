# ~5% (Later >10%) Existential Risk from Power-Seeking AI by 2070

## The Finding
Carlsmith estimates ~5% probability of existential catastrophe from misaligned power-seeking AI by 2070, based on multiplying subjective credences across six conditional premises. In a May 2022 update, this estimate was revised upward to >10%.

Premise breakdown: 65% × 80% × 40% × 65% × 40% × 95% ≈ 5%

Sensitivity tests yield a range of ~0.1%–40%.

## Source & Methodology
- [[sources/carlsmith-2021]] using [[methods/probabilistic-risk-decomposition]]

## Replication Status
**Not yet replicated** — this is a subjective probability estimate, not an empirical finding. Other researchers have produced their own estimates (e.g., Ord 2020 estimates 10% existential risk from unaligned AI overall), but using different methodologies and framings.

## Implications for the Taxonomy
This estimate treats [[concepts/power-seeking]] as the primary route to existential catastrophe from AI misalignment, distinct from other failure modes. The six-premise structure makes explicit which uncertainties contribute most to the overall risk estimate — practical PS-alignment difficulty (premise 3, 40%) and scaling to full disempowerment (premise 5, 40%) are the premises with lowest credence and thus the greatest leverage for reducing the estimate.
