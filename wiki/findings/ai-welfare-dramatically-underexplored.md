# AI Welfare and Multi-Agent Risks Are Dramatically Underexplored

## The Finding

AI welfare and rights represents less than 1% of all risks in the AI Risk Repository and appears in only 3% of the 65 reviewed documents. Multi-agent risks are similarly underexplored (3% of risks, 5% of documents). These are the two least-covered subdomains in the entire repository, despite their growing relevance as AI systems become more capable, autonomous, and numerous.

## Source & Methodology

- [[sources/slattery-et-al-2024]] using [[methods/best-fit-framework-synthesis]] — systematic coding of 1612 risks from 65 documents against a 24-subdomain Domain Taxonomy

## Replication Status

not yet replicated

## Implications for the Taxonomy

The underexploration of AI welfare connects to [[concepts/model-welfare]] and the emerging question of whether AI systems have morally relevant internal states. The underexploration of multi-agent risks connects to [[concepts/multi-agent-risks]] and is especially important as agentic AI deployment increases. These gaps suggest the wiki should prioritize tracking new sources on these topics.
