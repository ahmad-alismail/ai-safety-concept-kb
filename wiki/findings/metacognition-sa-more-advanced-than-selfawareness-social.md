# Meta-Cognition and Situational Awareness More Advanced Than Self-Awareness and Social Awareness in LLMs

## The Finding
Review of evaluation literature finds that meta-cognition and situational awareness have reached "relatively high levels of sophistication and reliability" in current LLMs, while self-awareness and social awareness "remain relatively rudimentary, lacking consistency and stability." Some researchers remain skeptical whether self-awareness and social awareness demonstrations reflect genuine awareness or sophisticated simulation.

## Source & Methodology
- [[sources/li-et-al-2025]] using review of empirical evaluations (SAD benchmark, false-belief tasks, knowledge boundary tests, CoT reasoning evaluations)

## Replication Status
Review synthesis — based on multiple independent evaluations.

## Implications for the Taxonomy
Suggests that [[concepts/ai-awareness]] sub-dimensions develop unevenly, with internal monitoring capabilities (meta-cognition) and environment understanding (SA) outpacing identity/social modeling. This asymmetry may shape which risks emerge first — SA-enabled risks (alignment faking, sandbagging) before social awareness-enabled risks (sophisticated ToM-based manipulation).
