# AI Risk Classifications Are Fragmented and Inconsistent

## The Finding

Existing AI risk classifications are uncoordinated and inconsistent. Analysis of 65 documents containing 1612 extracted risks reveals that no single document covers all 24 identified risk subdomains; the highest coverage was 19/24 (70%) and the average was only 8/24 (33%). Even when different classification systems use similar terms (e.g., "privacy"), they refer to concepts inconsistently. Most papers do not base their taxonomies on a conceptual or theoretical foundation, and most reviews are narrative rather than systematic.

## Source & Methodology

- [[sources/slattery-et-al-2024]] using [[methods/best-fit-framework-synthesis]] — systematic review of 17,288 records, 65 included documents, 1612 risks extracted and coded against dual taxonomy

## Replication Status

not yet replicated (first comprehensive meta-review of this kind)

## Implications for the Taxonomy

This finding directly motivates the wiki's mission of building a coherent ontology of AI safety concepts. The fragmentation documented by Slattery et al. confirms that the same concept is classified differently across sources (e.g., power-seeking as behavior vs. instrumental goal vs. socioeconomic risk). A unified framework with consistent definitions is a prerequisite for cumulative knowledge building in AI safety.
