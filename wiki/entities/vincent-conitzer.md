# Vincent Conitzer

## Profile
Professor at Carnegie Mellon University. Works on AI safety, multi-agent systems, and computational social choice. Supervisor on the Shi et al. deception taxonomy project.

## Key Contributions
- [[sources/shi-et-al-2026]] — Equal supervisor (with Zhijing Jin) of "From Hallucination to Scheming: A Unified Taxonomy and Benchmark Analysis for LLM Deception"
- [[sources/kovarik-et-al-2025]] — Corresponding author of "AI Testing Should Account for Sophisticated Strategic Behaviour" — position paper applying game theory to evaluation-based safety cases
- Kovařík, Oesterheld & Conitzer (2023) — "Game Theory with Simulation of Other Players" (IJCAI)

## Conceptual Framework
Advocates for game-theoretic formalisation of AI evaluation scenarios. Views the evaluator–AI interaction as a strategic game where testing can be analysed for robustness against misaligned strategic agents. Part of the Foundations of Cooperative AI Lab research program.
