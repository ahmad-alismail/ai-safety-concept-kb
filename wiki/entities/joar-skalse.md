# Joar Skalse

## Profile
Researcher in AI safety and reward learning. Affiliated with the Department of Computer Science at Oxford University and the Future of Humanity Institute (Oxford University).

## Key Contributions
- [[sources/skalse-et-al-2022]] — co-first author (with David Krueger) of the first formal definition of reward hacking; introduces [[concepts/hackability]] and [[concepts/simplification]] of reward functions
- [[sources/skalse-et-al-2023]] — co-first author of the invariance framework for reward learning partial identifiability

## Conceptual Framework
Focuses on the formal foundations of reward learning, characterizing what can and cannot be learned from various data sources. Treats reward functions as latent objects and analyzes the information-theoretic limits of reward inference.
