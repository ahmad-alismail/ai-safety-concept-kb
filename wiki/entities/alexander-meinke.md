# Alexander Meinke

## Profile
Researcher at [[entities/apollo-research|Apollo Research]]. Co-first author of the in-context scheming evaluation paper. Led the second half of the scheming project including evaluations for goal-guarding, covert email reranking, instrumental alignment faking, and sandbagging. Also contributed to the [[methods/sad-benchmark|SAD benchmark]] (developed the initial STAGES task).

## Key Contributions
- [[sources/meinke-et-al-2025]] — co-first author; led evaluations for deferred subversion and covert email reranking
- [[sources/laine-et-al-2024]] — contributor (developed initial STAGES task for SAD benchmark)
- [[sources/schoen-et-al-2025]] — responsible for Section 2 (requirements) and Section 7 (goal insertion experiment)

## Conceptual Framework
Focuses on empirical evaluation of AI scheming capabilities, distinguishing capability from propensity. Emphasizes importance of safety cases and scheming inability arguments.
