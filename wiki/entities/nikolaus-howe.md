# Nikolaus H. R. Howe

## Profile
AI safety researcher. Co-author of the first formal definition of reward hacking ([[sources/skalse-et-al-2022]]). Affiliated with Mila (Quebec AI Institute) and Université de Montréal.

## Key Contributions
- [[sources/skalse-et-al-2022]] — "Defining and Characterizing Reward Hacking" (co-author with Skalse, Krasheninnikov, Krueger)

## Conceptual Framework
Works in the formal-MDP tradition of reward-function analysis. The 2022 paper treats reward hacking as a structural property of a (true reward, proxy reward, policy set, environment) tuple — independent of agent psychology or situational awareness. Mathematical contributions span the proofs of unhackability impossibility on open policy sets (Theorem 1, Corollaries 1–2) and existence results for finite policy sets (Theorems 2–3, Corollary 3).
