# Willem Fourie

## Profile
Researcher at the School for Data Science and Computational Thinking, Stellenbosch University. Works at the intersection of philosophy and AI safety, applying Aristotelian metaphysics to AI alignment problems.

## Key Contributions
- [[sources/fourie-2025]] — "An Aristotelian Ontology of Instrumental Goals: Structural Features to Be Managed and Not Failures to Be Eliminated" (2025) — first ontological account of instrumental goals; dual-aspect framework (hypothetical necessity + accidental causation); argues for management rather than elimination

## Conceptual Framework
Fourie treats AI systems as complex artefacts in the Aristotelian sense — non-natural beings (*techneta*) whose ends are externally imposed through design, training, and deployment. Instrumental goals arise through two mechanisms: hypothetical necessity (structurally determined, robust) and accidental causation (contingent, unpredictable). This framework classifies instrumental goals as ontological features of imposed form rather than failure modes to be debugged.
