# Stuart Russell

## Profile
Professor of Computer Science at UC Berkeley. Pioneer of inverse reinforcement learning (Russell 1998) and author of the standard AI textbook (Russell & Norvig 2009). Leading voice on AI safety and alignment through the value alignment problem.

## Key Contributions
- [[sources/skalse-et-al-2023]] — co-author of the invariance framework for reward learning
- Inverse reinforcement learning (Russell 1998) — foundational to [[concepts/reward-learning]]
- Center for Human-Compatible Artificial Intelligence (CHAI), UC Berkeley

## Conceptual Framework
Advocates for AI systems that are aligned with human preferences through cooperative inverse reinforcement learning — learning what humans want rather than being told. Views the reward learning problem and its partial identifiability as central challenges for AI safety.
