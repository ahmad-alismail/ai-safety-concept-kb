# Simon Goldstein

## Profile
Researcher at Australian Catholic University and the Center for AI Safety. Co-lead author (with Peter S. Park) of the AI deception survey. Collaborated on the section about making AI systems less deceptive.

## Key Contributions
- [[sources/park-et-al-2023]] — co-lead author of "AI Deception: A Survey of Examples, Risks, and Potential Solutions"
- Goldstein & Kirk-Giannini (2023) — "AI Wellbeing" — argues that some AI systems may have beliefs and desires (cited in appendix of Park et al.)

## Conceptual Framework
Draws on functionalist philosophy of mind to argue that AI systems may possess beliefs and goals if they have appropriately complex functional capabilities. Applies this to the analysis of AI deception.
