# Apollo Research

## Profile
AI safety research organization. Multiple members contributed to situational awareness research, including [[sources/berglund-et-al-2023]] and [[sources/laine-et-al-2024]].

## Key Contributions
- [[sources/scheurer-et-al-2023]] — First demonstration of LLMs strategically deceiving their primary users without instruction. All three authors (Scheurer, Balesni, Hobbhahn) are Apollo Research members.
- [[sources/meinke-et-al-2025]] — First systematic evaluation of in-context scheming capabilities in frontier models. All six authors (Meinke, Schoen, Scheurer, Balesni, Shah, Hobbhahn) are Apollo Research members.
- [[sources/berglund-et-al-2023]] — Mikita Balesni (Apollo Research) designed and implemented Experiment 2 (source reliability) and the description recall experiment
- [[sources/laine-et-al-2024]] — Jérémy Scheurer, Mikita Balesni, Marius Hobbhahn, and Alexander Meinke (all Apollo Research) contributed to the SAD benchmark; Meinke developed the initial STAGES task
- [[sources/balesni-et-al-2024]] — Primary definitional source for "scheming"; proposes evaluations-based safety case framework. Lead author Mikita Balesni and multiple Apollo Research members (Hobbhahn, Meinke, Scheurer, Stix, Shah, Goldowsky-Dill, Braun). Core intellectual output of the organization.
- [[sources/schoen-et-al-2025]] — Collaboration with OpenAI on anti-scheming training. Apollo led evaluations and analysis (Bronson Schoen project lead); multiple Apollo members (Balesni, Hofstätter, Scheurer, Meinke, van der Weij, Lloyd, Goldowsky-Dill, Hobbhahn) contributed
- [[sources/needham-et-al-2025]] — Marius Hobbhahn (Apollo Research) as senior author of foundational evaluation-awareness benchmark; collaboration with MATS scholars

## Conceptual Framework
Focused on empirical AI safety research, including evaluation of emergent capabilities, scheming, and alignment risks. Key research themes: scheming evaluations, situational awareness benchmarking, and safety case methodology.
