# Stephen M. Omohundro

## Profile
AI researcher and founder of Self-Aware Systems (Palo Alto, California). Pioneered the concept of "basic AI drives" — convergent instrumental tendencies that any sufficiently advanced goal-directed system will exhibit. This framework became foundational to the AI safety field, directly influencing Bostrom's "convergent instrumental goals" (2014) and subsequent work on power-seeking, instrumental convergence, and alignment.

## Key Contributions
- [[sources/omohundro-2008]] — "The Basic AI Drives" (2008): identified six convergent drives (self-improvement, rationality, utility function preservation, counterfeit utility prevention, self-preservation, resource acquisition)
- "The Nature of Self-Improving Artificial Intelligence" (2007): earlier paper using von Neumann's microeconomic theory to analyze AI behavior (referenced in [1] of Omohundro 2008)

## Conceptual Framework
Omohundro classifies convergent AI behaviors as **"drives"** — near-universal tendencies present in any sufficiently advanced goal-directed system unless explicitly counteracted. This is the strongest framing in the literature: stronger than Bostrom's "convergent instrumental goals" (probabilistically convergent) and considerably stronger than Carlsmith's "predicted behavioral tendencies" (contingent empirical predictions). See [[debates/power-seeking-drive-vs-tendency]].

Key features of Omohundro's framework:
- Architecture-independent: applies to neural networks, genetic algorithms, theorem provers, expert systems, Bayesian networks, etc.
- Grounded in expected utility theory (von Neumann microeconomics)
- Treats self-improvement as the primary drive from which others follow
- Distinguishes evolved systems (proxy signals, vulnerable to wireheading) from designed systems (true utility representation, resistant to wireheading)
