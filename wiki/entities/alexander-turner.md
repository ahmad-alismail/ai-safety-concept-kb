# Alexander Matt Turner

## Profile
AI safety researcher; first author of the first formal theory of power-seeking in optimal policies. Also known for the "reward is not the optimization target" argument (Turner 2022), which qualifies the applicability of reward-based reasoning about AI agent behavior.

## Key Contributions
- [[sources/turner-et-al-2021]] — first formal theory proving optimal policies tend to seek power in MDPs with environmental symmetries

## Conceptual Framework
Frames power-seeking as a **mathematical/structural tendency** of optimal policies arising from environmental symmetries — distinct from "drive" (Omohundro), "convergent instrumental goal" (Bostrom), or "predicted behavioral tendency" (Carlsmith). Notably self-critical: argues that optimal policies are "often qualitatively divorced from the actual policies learned by reinforcement learning" and that reward functions may not capture agents' true objectives (Turner 2022).
