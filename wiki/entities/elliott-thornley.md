# Elliott Thornley

## Profile
Philosopher at the University of Oxford (Philosophy). Works on constructive decision theory and AI safety, particularly the shutdown problem and corrigibility.

## Key Contributions
- [[sources/thornley-2024]] — "The Shutdown Problem: An AI Engineering Puzzle for Decision Theorists" (forthcoming in *Philosophical Studies*): three formal theorems establishing the difficulty of the shutdown problem; introduces constructive decision theory framing
- [[sources/thornley-2026]] — "Shutdownable Agents through POST-Agency" (2025): full formal development of the POST-Agents Proposal; proves POST-agents are both shutdownable and useful; constructive counterpart to Thornley (2024a)
- Thornley (2024b) — "The Shutdown Problem: Incomplete Preferences as a Solution" (Alignment Forum): initial proposal of incomplete preferences as a solution direction; introduces training method
- Thornley et al. (2025) — "Towards Shutdownable Agents via Stochastic Choice" (TAIS 2025): empirical validation of POST training method in simple RL agents (with Roman, Ziakas, Ho, Thomson)

## Conceptual Framework
Advocates for "constructive decision theory" — a third branch of decision theory (alongside descriptive and normative) that concerns how we *want* artificial agents to behave and how to create agents that behave that way. Views the shutdown problem as a characteristic problem for this branch. Identifies formal trade-offs (discrimination–shutdownability, patience–shutdownability) and proposes violating Completeness via the POST-Agents Proposal as the constructive solution. The POST-Agents Proposal is a "third line of defence" for AI safety — if Full Alignment and Reliable Aversion fail, POST ensures agents are still shutdownable.
