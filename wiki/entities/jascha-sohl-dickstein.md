# Jascha Sohl-Dickstein

## Profile
Researcher at Anthropic. Originator of the "hot mess theory of intelligence" (2023 blog post) — the hypothesis that more intelligent agents behave less coherently. Co-corresponding author of Hägele et al. (2025), which provides the first empirical formalization and test of this theory.

## Key Contributions
- [[sources/sohl-dickstein-2023]] — originated the [[concepts/hot-mess-theory|hot mess theory of intelligence]] and the concept of [[concepts/supercoherence]]; conducted the first pilot experiment probing the intelligence-coherence relationship
- [[sources/hagele-et-al-2026]] — co-corresponding author; provided the theoretical framework empirically tested in the paper

## Conceptual Framework
Proposes that intelligence and coherence are not positively correlated by default — as entities become more intelligent, their behavior tends to become less well-described by a single goal. This challenges the standard assumption in AI safety that more capable systems will be more effective goal-pursuers.
