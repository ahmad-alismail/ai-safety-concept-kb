# Christian Tarsney

## Profile
Philosopher working on AI safety, decision theory, and instrumental convergence. Presented work at the 2024 "Language, Mind, and Ethics in AI" workshop at UT Austin.

## Key Contributions
- [[sources/tarsney-2025]] — formal decision-theoretic analysis of power-seeking as a convergent instrumental goal; four power formalizations; incompleteness result (Proposition 5); absolute power analysis

## Conceptual Framework
Tarsney approaches power-seeking and instrumental convergence through abstract decision theory rather than reinforcement learning. He formalizes power as relations on decision trees based on available lottery sets, defines instrumental convergence via the behavior of "random agents" (expected utility maximizers with randomly generated utility functions), and proves both positive results (power is convergent) and negative results (power rankings are necessarily incomplete). This framework is more general than the MDP-based approach of Turner et al. (2021) but yields both stronger and weaker conclusions.
