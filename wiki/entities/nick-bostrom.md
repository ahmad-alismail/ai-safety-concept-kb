# Nick Bostrom

## Profile
Philosopher and founding Director of the Future of Humanity Institute (FHI) at the University of Oxford. Professor in the Faculty of Philosophy & Oxford Martin School. One of the most influential thinkers in AI existential risk. His book *Superintelligence* (2014) established the conceptual vocabulary used across the AI safety field.

## Key Contributions
- [[sources/bostrom-2012]] — "The Superintelligent Will: Motivation and Instrumental Rationality in Advanced Artificial Agents" (*Minds and Machines*, 2012) — original journal article introducing the orthogonality thesis and instrumental convergence thesis with five convergent instrumental values
- [[sources/bostrom-2014]] — *Superintelligence: Paths, Dangers, Strategies* (2014) — book expanding on the 2012 paper and other work

## Conceptual Framework
Bostrom organizes AI safety concepts hierarchically:
1. **Meta-theoretical principles**: [[concepts/orthogonality-thesis]], [[concepts/instrumental-convergence]] thesis
2. **Convergent instrumental values**: [[concepts/self-preservation]], [[concepts/goal-content-integrity]], cognitive enhancement, technological perfection, [[concepts/resource-acquisition]] — explicitly uses "instrumental values" rather than Omohundro's "drives"
3. **Failure modes**: [[concepts/treacherous-turn]], [[concepts/perverse-instantiation]], [[concepts/infrastructure-profusion]], [[concepts/mind-crime]]
4. **System types**: Speed/collective/quality [[concepts/superintelligence-forms]]; oracle/genie/sovereign/tool-AI
5. **Control methods**: Capability control (boxing, incentive methods, stunting, tripwires) + Motivation selection (direct specification, domesticity, [[concepts/indirect-normativity]], augmentation)
6. **Value alignment**: [[concepts/value-loading-problem]], [[concepts/coherent-extrapolated-volition]]
