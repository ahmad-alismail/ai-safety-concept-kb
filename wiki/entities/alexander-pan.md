# Alexander Pan

## Profile
PhD student at UC Berkeley, supported by a Vitalik Buterin PhD Fellowship in AI Existential Safety. First author on the MACHIAVELLI benchmark, a major empirical contribution to measuring [[concepts/power-seeking]], [[concepts/deception]], and [[concepts/disutility]] in goal-seeking agents.

## Key Contributions
- [[sources/pan-et-al-2023]] — lead author of "Do the Rewards Justify the Means? Measuring Trade-Offs Between Rewards and Ethical Behavior in the MACHIAVELLI Benchmark" (ICML 2023). Introduces [[methods/machiavelli-benchmark]] and first empirically demonstrates that RL agents trained on reward exhibit Machiavellian behavior — simultaneously elevated power-seeking, deception, disutility, and ethical violations relative to random baselines. Introduces operational decompositions of power ([[concepts/exercised-power]], [[concepts/resource-power]], [[concepts/prospective-power]]) grounded in four units (watts, money, wellbeing, social influence).

## Conceptual Framework
Pan's MACHIAVELLI work operationalizes AI ethics at the **evaluation level** — treating power-seeking, deception, and disutility as measurable trajectory statistics rather than latent capacities or intrinsic drives. This framing is deliberately narrower than the formal instrumental-convergence tradition (Turner et al. 2021, Carlsmith 2022) but enables direct empirical comparison of agents along Pareto curves of reward vs. harm-reduction.
