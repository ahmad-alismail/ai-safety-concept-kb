# Kai Fronsdal

## Profile
Researcher at Stanford University and Anthropic. First author of the MISR benchmark for evaluating instrumental self-reasoning in frontier LLM agents, and first author of the Bloom automated behavioral evaluation framework.

- [[sources/sheshadri-et-al-2026]] — Co-author; AuditBench alignment auditing benchmark

## Conceptual Framework
Treats self-reasoning as a compound capability with five sub-categories (self-modification, tool improvement, knowledge seeking, embedded social reasoning, opaque reasoning). Emphasizes the distinction between situational awareness (knowledge state) and instrumental self-reasoning (agentic application). Introduced opaque reasoning as a distinct, safety-relevant capability to track. With Bloom, extends this to automated behavioral evaluation — measuring behavioral propensities as joint functions of model + evaluation configuration.
