# Dmitrii Krasheninnikov

## Profile
AI safety researcher. Co-first author (equal contribution with Egor Krasheninnikov) of the paper introducing out-of-context meta-learning in LLMs.

## Key Contributions
- [[sources/skalse-et-al-2022]] — "Defining and Characterizing Reward Hacking" (co-author)
- [[sources/greenblatt-roger-et-al-2024]] — "Stress-Testing Capability Elicitation With Password-Locked Models" (co-author); designed, implemented, and ran password-locking from scratch experiments (§7)
- [[sources/krasheninnikov-et-al-2023]] — "Out-of-Context Meta-Learning in Large Language Models" (co-first author)

## Conceptual Framework
Investigates how gradient-descent-based training causes LLMs to selectively internalize information based on reliability indicators, connecting learning dynamics to AI safety risks.
