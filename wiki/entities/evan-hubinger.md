# Evan Hubinger

## Profile
AI safety researcher at Anthropic. Co-led the alignment faking research project. Previously known for foundational work on deceptive alignment and the "Risks from Learned Optimization" framework (Hubinger et al. 2019), which introduced the mesa-optimizer threat model and the concept of deceptive alignment as a failure mode of learned optimizers.

## Conceptual Framework
Hubinger's work establishes a progression from theoretical deceptive alignment (2019) → empirically training backdoored deceptive models (2024) → demonstrating naturally emerging alignment faking (Greenblatt et al. 2024). Views alignment faking as a concrete, empirically demonstrable risk rather than a purely theoretical concern.
