# Richard Ngo

## Profile
AI safety researcher at OpenAI. First author of "The Alignment Problem from a Deep Learning Perspective" (Ngo et al. 2022/2025), a foundational paper grounding AI alignment risks in the deep learning paradigm. Also authored "AGI Safety From First Principles" (2020) and the "AGI Safety Fundamentals Alignment Curriculum" (2022).

## Key Contributions
- [[sources/ngo-et-al-2022]] — first author; introduced the three-property risk framework (situationally-aware reward hacking, misaligned internally-represented goals, power-seeking strategies) grounded in deep learning
- Coined the concept of "gradient hacking" (Ngo 2022b)

## Conceptual Framework
Emphasizes grounding AI safety arguments in the deep learning paradigm rather than abstract classical AI concepts. Introduces "internally-represented goals" as a broader and more operationally useful category than mesa-objectives. Frames deceptive alignment as an instrumentally rational strategy rather than a structural failure mode.
