# Ethan Perez

## Profile
AI safety researcher at Anthropic. Focus on evaluating and understanding language model behaviors, including automated evaluation generation and red teaming.

## Key Contributions
- [[sources/perez-et-al-2022-red-teaming]] — led research on automated LM-based red teaming at DeepMind/NYU; introduced four-method test case generation pipeline
- [[sources/perez-et-al-2022]] — led the project on model-written evaluations; discovered inverse scaling of sycophancy and instrumental subgoal expression with model size and RLHF
- [[sources/benton-et-al-2024]] — co-author of sabotage evaluations framework for frontier models
- [[sources/jones-et-al-2025]] — co-author of Gumbel-tail forecasting method for rare behavior prediction
- [[sources/lynch-et-al-2025]] — equal advising; agentic misalignment red-teaming across 16 frontier models

## Conceptual Framework
Pioneered the use of LMs to evaluate LMs at scale. Treats AI safety concepts (sycophancy, instrumental subgoals, situational awareness) as measurable behavioral tendencies — operationalized as what models say they would do in response to evaluation prompts. Emphasizes the distinction between stated desires and actual behavior while arguing that stated desires "suggest that models may act in accord with potentially dangerous subgoals."
