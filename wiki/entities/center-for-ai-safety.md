# Center for AI Safety (CAIS)

## Profile
AI safety research organization. Organized the widely-signed "Statement on AI Risk" (2023) declaring that "mitigating the risk of extinction from AI should be a global priority." Provides resources and funding for AI safety research.

## Key Contributions
- [[sources/park-et-al-2023]] — affiliation of Goldstein, O'Gara, Chen, and Hendrycks
- [[sources/ren-et-al-2024]] — "Safetywashing" (NeurIPS 2024) — primary affiliation for Ren, Basart, Khoja, Gatti, Phan, Yin, Mazeika, Kim, Fitz, and Hendrycks. Introduces capabilities-correlation meta-analysis of safety benchmarks.
- Statement on AI Risk (2023) — signed by experts across academia and industry
- [[sources/pan-et-al-2023]] — MACHIAVELLI benchmark (ICML 2023). CAIS authors: Chan, Basart, Ng, Hendrycks. Evaluates [[concepts/machiavellianism]] — joint power-seeking, deception, disutility, and ethical-violation behaviors — in text-based AI agents via [[methods/machiavelli-benchmark]].
- WMDP benchmark (Li et al. 2024) — weapons-of-mass-destruction knowledge unlearning benchmark, subsequently audited in Ren et al. 2024 and found strongly negatively correlated with capabilities on both base and chat models.
- [[sources/krishna-et-al-2025]] — D-REX deceptive-reasoning benchmark (2025); CAIS affiliation of Zou and Hendrycks.
