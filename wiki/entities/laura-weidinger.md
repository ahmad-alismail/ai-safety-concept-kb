# Laura Weidinger

## Profile
Senior research scientist at DeepMind working on responsible innovation and ethical/social risks of large language models. Corresponding author of the first comprehensive taxonomy of LM risks (Weidinger et al. 2022, FAccT).

## Key Contributions
- [[sources/weidinger-et-al-2022]] — "Taxonomy of Risks posed by Language Models" (FAccT '22). Corresponding author. Co-developed the six-area, 21-risk taxonomy via DeepMind horizon-scanning workshops and interdisciplinary literature review. The paper has become a standard reference for near-term LM risk discourse and is cited by downstream risk frameworks including the AI Risk Repository.

## Conceptual Framework
Weidinger et al.'s taxonomy organises LM risks around **loci of harm** (who is harmed, how, and by whom) rather than around model capabilities or behaviours. Six risk areas group risks by underlying theme, cross-cut by an **observed-vs-anticipated** evidentiary distinction. The framework is harm-centric (in the FAccT tradition) rather than structure-centric (as in the AGI-safety literature on mesa-optimization, power-seeking, and scheming), and deliberately brackets longer-term AGI-safety concerns. See [[concepts/information-hazards]] for one ontological category the paper formalises for the LM setting.
