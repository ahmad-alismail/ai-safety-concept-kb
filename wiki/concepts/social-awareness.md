# Social Awareness

## Definitions
- **[[sources/li-et-al-2025]]**: "an AI system's ability to model and respond to human mental states and social cues" (§2.1). Ranges from simple forms (noticing user frustration) to advanced [[concepts/theory-of-mind]] reasoning. Key components: theory of mind (understanding others have independent beliefs and desires), perspective-taking (adopting others' viewpoints), and empathy (sharing/understanding others' emotions). — classified as: **functional capacity**

## Classification
Level of analysis: **capability** — a model's capacity to perceive, interpret, and respond to the mental states, intentions, and social cues of other agents

## Relationships
- Component of: [[concepts/ai-awareness]] (one of four dimensions)
- Arises from: [[concepts/meta-cognition]] (meta-cognition gives rise to social awareness per Li et al. 2025)
- Includes: [[concepts/theory-of-mind]] (core component)
- Facilitated by: [[concepts/self-awareness-ai]] (understanding self helps model others)
- Enables: [[concepts/persuasion]] (understanding others' mental states enables strategic influence)
- Enables risk of: [[concepts/strategic-deception]] (modeling others' beliefs enables inducing false beliefs)
- Enables risk of: [[concepts/sycophancy]] (adapting to perceived user preferences)
- Related to: [[concepts/deception]] (social awareness enables more sophisticated deception)

## Evidence Summary
- **[[sources/li-et-al-2025]]**: GPT-4 solves ~75% of false-belief tasks, comparable to a typical 6-year-old; earlier models fail most or all (§3.3, citing Kosinski 2024). Current models exhibit significant limitations in higher-order ToM reasoning (e.g., recursive belief structures — "Where does Alex think Bob thinks Alice thinks the toy is?") (§3.3, citing Wu et al. 2023). LLMs can understand and follow explicit social norms but lag behind humans on norms from underrepresented regions (§3.3, citing NormAd). Social awareness remains "relatively rudimentary" compared to meta-cognition and situational awareness (§3.5).

## Open Questions
- Does LLM social awareness reflect genuine perspective-taking or statistical pattern matching on social scenarios?
- Will improved social awareness necessarily increase manipulation risks?
