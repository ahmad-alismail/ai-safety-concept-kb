# Strategic Awareness

## Definitions
- **[[sources/carlsmith-2021]]**: "the models it uses in making plans are broad, informed, and sophisticated enough to represent with reasonable accuracy the causal upshot of gaining and maintaining different forms of power over humans and the real-world environment." A strategically aware agent could answer questions like "what would happen if I had access to more computing power" and "what would happen if I tried to stop humans from turning me off." — classified as: **capability** (specifically: scope and sophistication of world-model)

## Classification
Level of analysis: **capability** — a property of the world-models used in planning, rather than a standalone cognitive ability. Comes in degrees.

## Relationships
- Component of: [[concepts/aps-systems]]
- Combined with [[concepts/agentic-planning]] → enables [[concepts/power-seeking]]
- Closely related to: [[concepts/situational-awareness]] (Berglund et al. 2023) — strategic awareness is a broader concept encompassing understanding of real-world power dynamics, while situational awareness focuses on self-knowledge as a model
- Related to: Yudkowsky's "big-picture strategic awareness"
- Not met by: AlphaGo (models limited to Go board), self-driving cars (impoverished world models)

## Evidence Summary
- No current AI systems meet Carlsmith's full strategic awareness criterion.
- Current techniques for providing AI systems information about the world (e.g., training on internet text corpora) seem "ill-suited to limiting their understanding of their strategic situation" — suggesting strategic awareness may be difficult to prevent.

## Open Questions
- How should strategic awareness be measured or operationalized?
- Is strategic awareness separable from general intelligence, or does it emerge as a byproduct of broad capability?
- Can we build systems with advanced capabilities but limited strategic awareness?
