# Theory of Mind

## Definitions
- **[[sources/li-et-al-2025]]**: "the ability to attribute beliefs, desires, and knowledge distinct from one's own" (§3.3). Typically assessed through false-belief tasks, which require modeling another agent's mental state. — classified as: **cognitive capacity / component of social awareness**
- **[[sources/shi-et-al-2026]]**: Identified as one of three prerequisites for [[concepts/strategic-deception]] (alongside [[concepts/goal-directedness]] and [[concepts/situational-awareness]]): "strategic deception requires representing others' beliefs and how those beliefs might be influenced. LLMs demonstrate substantial theory-of-mind capabilities in benchmark tasks, though robustness remains debated" (Section D.1). — classified as: **capability** (prerequisite for strategic deception)

## Classification
Level of analysis: **capability** — a core component of [[concepts/social-awareness]]; the ability to model others' mental states as distinct from one's own

## Relationships
- Component of: [[concepts/social-awareness]]
- Enables: [[concepts/strategic-deception]] (modeling others' beliefs enables deliberately inducing false beliefs — Hagendorff 2024)
- Enables: [[concepts/persuasion]] (understanding others' mental states supports influence)
- Related to: [[concepts/deception]] (advanced ToM is prerequisite for sophisticated deception)
- Assessed via: false-belief tasks, recursive belief reasoning tasks

## Evidence Summary
- **[[sources/li-et-al-2025]]**: GPT-4 solves ~75% of false-belief tasks (comparable to typical 6-year-old), whereas earlier models like GPT-3 fail most or all (§3.3, citing Kosinski 2024). Higher-order ToM reasoning (recursive belief structures) remains significantly limited even in GPT-4 (§3.3, citing Wu et al. 2023). LLMs outperform humans on certain social situational judgment scenarios (§3.3, citing Mittelstädt et al. 2024). Hagendorff (2024) provides empirical evidence that GPT-4 can understand false belief induction and cause simulated agents to believe falsehoods (§5.1).

## Open Questions
- Does LLM performance on ToM tasks reflect genuine mental state modeling or exploitation of surface-level linguistic patterns?
- At what model scale does ToM become reliable enough to enable sophisticated strategic deception?
