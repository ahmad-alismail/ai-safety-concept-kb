# Seed AI

## Definitions
- **[[sources/bostrom-2014]]**: An AI capable of improving its own architecture — not just accumulating content but engineering new algorithms and computational structures. Distinguished from Turing's "child machine" by this self-modifying capacity. A seed AI is the starting point for an [[concepts/intelligence-explosion]]: once it reaches sufficient capability, the recursive self-improvement feedback loop activates. (Ch. 2) — classified as: **path to superintelligence / system type**

## Classification
Level of analysis: **system type** — a specific kind of AI system that enables [[concepts/recursive-self-improvement]] and potentially triggers an [[concepts/intelligence-explosion]].

## Relationships
- Enables: [[concepts/recursive-self-improvement]] → [[concepts/intelligence-explosion]]
- Path to: [[concepts/superintelligence-forms]]
- Subject to: [[concepts/control-problem]] (must be solved before seed AI reaches superintelligence)
- Related to: [[concepts/ai-development-capability]] (a seed AI possesses this dangerous capability by definition)

## Evidence Summary
- Theoretical concept (Bostrom 2014). No seed AI has been demonstrated. Current AI systems show early forms of self-improvement (architecture search, code generation for AI research) but lack the full recursive self-modification capacity Bostrom describes.

## Open Questions
- Would a seed AI need to be a unified system, or could the recursive improvement dynamic emerge from an ecosystem of AI tools?
- Can governance mechanisms prevent uncontrolled seed AI development?
