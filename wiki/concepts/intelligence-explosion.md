# Intelligence Explosion

## Definitions
- **I.J. Good (1965)** (as cited in [[sources/bostrom-2014]]): "Let an ultraintelligent machine be defined as a machine that can far surpass all the intellectual activities of any man however clever. Since the design of machines is one of these intellectual activities, an ultraintelligent machine could design even better machines; there would then unquestionably be an 'intelligence explosion,' and the intelligence of man would be left far behind."
- **[[sources/bostrom-2014]]**: Develops Good's concept into a full kinetic analysis. The rate of intelligence change = Optimization Power / Recalcitrance. Positive feedback loop: as the system improves, its optimization power increases, which further reduces recalcitrance. Three takeoff speeds: slow (decades–centuries), moderate (months–years), fast (minutes–days). Bostrom argues explosive takeoff is most probable. (Ch. 4) — classified as: **dynamic process / recursive self-improvement cascade**

## Classification
Level of analysis: **process/scenario** — the dynamic by which an AI system rapidly transitions from human-level to superintelligent capability through [[concepts/recursive-self-improvement]].

## Relationships
- Enabled by: [[concepts/seed-ai]] + [[concepts/recursive-self-improvement]]
- Accelerated by: hardware overhang, content overhang (pre-made knowledge exploitable at human parity)
- Leads to: [[concepts/decisive-strategic-advantage]] (in fast/moderate takeoff)
- Governed by: optimization power (quality-weighted design effort) and recalcitrance (resistance to improvement)
- Related to: [[concepts/superintelligence-forms]] (the endpoint of an intelligence explosion)

## Evidence Summary
- Theoretical argument (Good 1965, Bostrom 2014). No empirical demonstration of a full intelligence explosion.
- Current precursors: AI systems accelerating AI research (architecture search, code generation, chip design) — noted by [[sources/ngo-et-al-2022]].

## Open Questions
- Will recalcitrance increase fast enough to prevent explosive takeoff?
- Does the optimization power feedback loop apply to neural networks trained via gradient descent, or only to explicitly self-modifying systems?
