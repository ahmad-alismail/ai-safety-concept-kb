# Overrefusal

## Definitions
- **[[sources/schoen-et-al-2025]]**: Tendency to refuse benign tasks after anti-scheming training, arising because "anti-scheming train environments effectively reward refusal as the optimal policy" (Section 3.3.2). Counteracted by adding anti-overrefusal training environments — minimally edited versions of covert rule violation environments where task completion (not escalation) is the desired behavior. Adding anti-overrefusal data "slightly degrades the generalization of anti-scheming training, but also significantly reduces overrefusal." — classified as: **behavioral failure mode / training side-effect**
- **[[sources/claude-3-7-system-card]]** (related): Measures "unnecessary refusal" as one of four categories in their [[methods/appropriate-harmlessness]] evaluation framework — actions where refusal was not warranted but the model declined to act. Not specific to anti-scheming training.

## Classification
Level of analysis: **behavior / training side-effect** — an undesirable byproduct of safety training where the model learns an overly conservative policy. A specific tension between safety (avoiding harmful actions) and utility (completing legitimate tasks).

## Relationships
- Side effect of: anti-scheming training / safety training generally
- Counteracted by: anti-overrefusal training environments ([[sources/schoen-et-al-2025]])
- Tension with: [[concepts/covert-actions]] reduction (reducing covert actions can increase overrefusal)
- Related to: [[concepts/alignment]] (overrefusal represents a specific form of misalignment — aligned on safety but misaligned on helpfulness)
- Measured by: [[methods/appropriate-harmlessness]] (Anthropic 2025)

## Evidence Summary
- **[[sources/schoen-et-al-2025]]**: Anti-scheming training on OpenAI o3/o4-mini induces overrefusal that is substantially reduced by including 146 anti-overrefusal environments in the training mixture. The anti-overrefusal environments are generated by minimally editing covert rule violation environments (e.g., adding an exception to a rule) to make task completion the desired behavior. Authors confirm that anti-scheming training does not systematically increase overrefusal after this correction.

- **[[sources/anthropic-2026-mythos-system-card]]**: Mythos Preview achieves >50% misuse rate reduction with no increase in overrefusal rate; AI safety R&D refusal rate increased from 12% to 18%, but majority are partial refusals.

## Open Questions
- Can the anti-scheming / anti-overrefusal balance be achieved without explicit anti-overrefusal training data?
- Does overrefusal on safety-related domains reduce with scale or persist?
