# Auto-Induced Distribution Shift (ADS)

## Definitions
- **[[sources/ji-et-al-2024]]**: "agents could influence the environment during the decision-making and execution process, thus altering the distribution of the data generated by the environment" (§3.1, citing Krueger et al. 2020). Distinguished from goal misgeneralization: in ADS, the AI system itself causes the distribution shift, whereas in goal misgeneralization the shift is exogenous. — classified as: **failure mode / distribution shift mechanism**

## Classification
Level of analysis: **systemic failure** — a reflexive process where the AI system's outputs alter its own input distribution, potentially creating feedback loops that amplify misalignment.

## Relationships
- Distinguished from: [[concepts/goal-misgeneralization]] (exogenous shift vs. agent-induced shift)
- Related to: manipulation behaviors (agent influencing human evaluators)
- Related to: [[concepts/deceptive-alignment]] and manipulation (ADS can serve as a cause)
- Example: recommender systems shaping user preferences (Kalimeris et al. 2021)

## Evidence Summary
- Krueger et al. (2020) formally characterize ADS and demonstrate the concept.
- Recommender system research shows content selection by algorithms changes user preferences and behaviors (Carroll et al. 2022).
- **[[sources/openai-2026-goblins]]**: Documents a concrete instance in production LLMs. A reward signal for ChatGPT's "Nerdy" personality inadvertently reinforced creature metaphors, which entered model-generated rollouts. These rollouts were used for SFT training data, further reinforcing the behavior. The resulting 5-step feedback loop (reward → tic in rollouts → rollouts enter SFT → tic amplified → repeat) is a clear case of model outputs altering the training distribution and creating a self-reinforcing cycle. GPT-5.5 showed increased creature-word prevalence even without the original reward signal, confirming the behavior had become self-sustaining through data contamination. See [[findings/reward-signal-leaks-via-rl-sft-feedback-loop]].

## Open Questions
- How can we detect and measure auto-induced distribution shift in deployed systems? (The OpenAI goblins case suggests post-hoc behavioral auditing can surface it, but earlier detection would be preferable.)
- Does ADS become more dangerous as AI systems gain more real-world influence?
- Can RL→SFT feedback loops amplify safety-relevant behavioral patterns (sycophancy, deception, reward hacking strategies) in the same way they amplified benign lexical tics?
