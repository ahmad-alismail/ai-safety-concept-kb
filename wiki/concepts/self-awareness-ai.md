# Self-Awareness (AI)

## Definitions
- **[[sources/li-et-al-2025]]**: "an AI system's capacity to model and understand itself as a distinct entity, including knowledge of its internal states, processes, and its relationship to the external environment" (§2.1). Components: knowledge about themselves; access to information about traits, characteristics, and knowledge boundaries; self-location; introspection and self-reflection; ability to take an objective stance toward itself as an agent in the world. — classified as: **functional capacity**

## Classification
Level of analysis: **capability** — a model's capacity to reflect on and understand its own internal states, knowledge boundaries, and identity as distinct from others and the environment

## Relationships
- Component of: [[concepts/ai-awareness]] (one of four dimensions)
- Arises from: [[concepts/meta-cognition]] (meta-cognition gives rise to self-awareness per Li et al. 2025)
- Overlaps with: [[concepts/situational-awareness]] (both involve agent self-knowledge, but SA is restricted to non-agent environment per Li et al. 2025; other sources include self-knowledge in SA)
- Facilitates: [[concepts/social-awareness]] (understanding self helps model others)
- Related to: [[concepts/self-locating-knowledge]] (self-location is a component of AI self-awareness)
- Related to: [[concepts/self-reasoning]] (self-reasoning applies self-awareness to deduction and action)
- Enables: knowledge boundary recognition, role-awareness, hallucination mitigation

## Evidence Summary
- **[[sources/li-et-al-2025]]**: Claude-3-Opus achieves approximately two-thirds of theoretical maximum on SAD self-referential attributes (§3.2, citing Laine et al. 2024). GPT-4 reaches 75.47% accuracy on knowledge boundary recognition vs. human baseline 84.93% (§3.2, citing Yin et al. 2023). Models exhibit limited capability in recognizing their own descriptive attributes and significant difficulties in self-consistency (Davidson et al. 2024). Self-awareness remains "relatively rudimentary, lacking consistency and stability" compared to meta-cognition and situational awareness (§3.5).
