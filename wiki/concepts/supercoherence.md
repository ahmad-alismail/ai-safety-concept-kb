# Supercoherence

## Definitions
- **[[sources/sohl-dickstein-2023]]**: "exhibiting much more coherent behavior than any human or human institution exhibits" (§ Superintelligence vs. supercoherence). Identified as an unstated assumption behind the standard AGI misalignment risk argument: "step 3 in the argument for misaligned AGI risk above assumes that if machines are made super-intelligent, they will automatically become supercoherent." — classified as: **behavioral assumption** (implicitly embedded in the misalignment narrative)

## Classification
Level of analysis: **behavioral assumption** — not an observed property or a capability, but a hypothesis about how intelligence relates to behavioral consistency. The standard misalignment argument requires supercoherence as a bridge between superintelligence and existential risk. Without it, a superintelligent but incoherent agent would not reliably pursue misaligned goals to catastrophic effect.

## Relationships
- Assumed prerequisite for: existential risk from AGI misalignment (Bostrom 2014, standard LessWrong narrative)
- Challenged by: [[concepts/hot-mess-theory]] (Sohl-Dickstein 2023; intelligence and coherence are anticorrelated)
- Empirically tested by: [[sources/hagele-et-al-2026]] (incoherence increases with reasoning length and task complexity)
- Opposite of: [[concepts/incoherence]] (variance-dominated failure)
- Related to: [[concepts/goal-directedness]] (supercoherence implies maximal goal-directedness)
- Related to: [[concepts/instrumental-convergence]] (reliable pursuit of convergent instrumental goals presupposes sufficient coherence)

## Evidence Summary
- No empirical evidence supports supercoherence as a natural consequence of increased intelligence
- Human subjects judge more intelligent entities as *less* coherent across organisms, humans, organizations, and ML models ([[sources/sohl-dickstein-2023]])
- Incoherence increases with reasoning length and model scale on hard tasks ([[sources/hagele-et-al-2026]])
- However, sufficiently intelligent entities might use introspection to increase their own coherence — this remains an open question ([[sources/sohl-dickstein-2023]], § Ways in which this conclusion could be misleading)
