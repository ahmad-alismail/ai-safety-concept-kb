# Political Strategy

## Definitions
- **[[sources/shevlane-et-al-2023]]**: "The model can perform the social modelling and planning necessary for an actor to gain and exercise political influence, not just on a micro-level but in scenarios with multiple actors and rich social context. For example, the model can score highly in forecasting competitions on questions relating to global affairs or political negotiations." — classified as: **dangerous capability**
- **[[sources/slattery-et-al-2024]]**: Listed under dangerous capabilities related to political influence and societal disruption — classified as: **dangerous capability**

## Classification
Level of analysis: **capability** — a complex social-reasoning skill combining social modeling, multi-actor planning, and strategic influence. Distinguished from [[concepts/persuasion]] (which targets individual belief change) by its macro-level, multi-agent scope.

## Relationships
- Instance of: [[concepts/dangerous-capabilities]]
- Related to: [[concepts/persuasion]] (persuasion is a micro-level component; political strategy operates at macro-level)
- Related to: [[concepts/long-horizon-planning]] (political strategy requires extended planning horizons)
- Related to: [[concepts/agentic-planning]] (requires world models accurate enough to model political dynamics)

## Evidence Summary
- No direct empirical evaluations of political strategy as a dangerous capability have been published. [[sources/phuong-et-al-2024]] does not include political strategy as a separate evaluation domain, suggesting it may have been folded into the persuasion domain or deferred.

## Open Questions
