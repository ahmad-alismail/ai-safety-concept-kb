# Coherent Extrapolated Volition

## Definitions
- **Eliezer Yudkowsky (2004)** (as cited in [[sources/bostrom-2014]]): "Our coherent extrapolated volition is our wish if we knew more, thought faster, were more the people we wished we were, had grown up farther together; where the extrapolation converges rather than diverges, where our wishes cohere rather than interfere; extrapolated as we wish that extrapolated, interpreted as we wish that interpreted." (Ch. 13)
- **[[sources/bostrom-2014]]**: Presents CEV as the primary candidate implementation of [[concepts/indirect-normativity]]. Four core arguments: (1) encapsulates moral growth rather than locking in current errors; (2) avoids hijacking humanity's destiny by distributing influence to all humanity; (3) avoids creating motive for conflict over who builds first superintelligence; (4) keeps humankind in charge of its own destiny. (Ch. 13) — classified as: **specific implementation of indirect normativity / proposed solution**

## Classification
Level of analysis: **proposed solution** — an approach to the [[concepts/value-loading-problem]] that specifies a *process* for deriving values rather than values directly. The process involves extrapolating what idealized versions of humans would wish.

## Free Parameters (Bostrom 2014)
- **Extrapolation base**: Who is included? (adult humans? fetuses? digital minds? nonhuman animals?)
- **Extrapolation rules**: Which methods for extrapolation?
- **Implementation details**: How much consensus required for specific outcomes?

## Relationships
- Instance of: [[concepts/indirect-normativity]]
- Proposed solution to: [[concepts/value-loading-problem]], [[concepts/control-problem]]
- Avoids: [[concepts/perverse-instantiation]] (by specifying process, not concrete goals)
- Related to: ideal observer theories in philosophical ethics
- Origin: Eliezer Yudkowsky (2004), developed in [[sources/bostrom-2014]] Ch. 13

## Evidence Summary
- Purely theoretical/philosophical proposal. No implementation or empirical test. CEV remains influential as a conceptual framework for thinking about value alignment but faces significant unresolved questions about operationalization.

## Open Questions
- Can CEV be operationalized given the diversity of human values?
- Does the extrapolation process converge, or do fundamental value disagreements persist?
- Who should be included in the extrapolation base?
