# Forms of Superintelligence

## Definitions
- **[[sources/bostrom-2014]]**: Defines three forms of superintelligence (Ch. 3):
  - **Speed superintelligence**: "A system that can do all that a human intellect can do but much faster" — orders of magnitude speedup (10,000× to 1,000,000×). Most straightforwardly instantiated via [[concepts/whole-brain-emulation]].
  - **Collective superintelligence**: "A system composed of a large number of smaller intellects such that the system's overall performance across many very general domains vastly outstrips that of any current cognitive system." Excels at parallelizable problems.
  - **Quality superintelligence**: "A system that is at least as fast as a human mind and vastly qualitatively smarter." The difference between human and quality superintelligence is like the difference between human and animal cognition — qualitative, not merely quantitative.
  
  All three forms have equivalent **indirect reach** (each can develop technology to create the other forms), but differ in **direct reach** (immediate capability on different task types). — classified as: **system type taxonomy**

## Classification
Level of analysis: **taxonomic framework** — organizes different ways superintelligent capability could manifest.
