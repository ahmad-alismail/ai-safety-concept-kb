# Singleton

## Definitions
- **[[sources/bostrom-2012]]**: "A superintelligent agent that faces no significant intelligent rivals or opposition, and is thus in a position to determine global policy unilaterally." Used in the context of analyzing convergent instrumental values: a superintelligent singleton "would have instrumental reason to perfect the technologies that would make it better able to shape the world according to its preferred designs." (§2.4) — classified as: **strategic/governance concept**
- **[[sources/bostrom-2014]]**: "A sufficiently internally coordinated political structure with no external opponents." Broader definition than the 2012 version — not limited to superintelligent agents. A superintelligent agent achieving a [[concepts/decisive-strategic-advantage]] would likely form a singleton that "could determine the disposition of humanity's cosmic endowment." Even multipolar beginnings could coalesce into a singleton through long-term political integration or one agent gaining advantage. (Ch. 5, 11) — classified as: **post-transition political structure / governance outcome**

## Classification
Level of analysis: **governance outcome** — describes the end state of a successful [[concepts/intelligence-explosion]] where a single decision-making agency controls global policy.

## Relationships
- Produced by: [[concepts/decisive-strategic-advantage]]
- Preceded by: [[concepts/intelligence-explosion]] (fast/moderate takeoff)
- Alternative to: multipolar scenarios (Ch. 11 of [[sources/bostrom-2014]])
- Characterized by: no external opponents; determines future of Earth-originating life
- Values depend on: solving [[concepts/control-problem]] and [[concepts/value-loading-problem]]
- Related to: wise-singleton sustainability threshold — a capability level that is "quite low" (even contemporary humanity with patience and risk-savvy could meet it)

## Evidence Summary
- Purely theoretical (Bostrom 2014). The concept extends political science ideas about world government to post-superintelligence scenarios.

## Open Questions
- Is singleton formation inevitable after superintelligence, or could stable multipolar equilibria persist?
- Would a singleton controlled by a superintelligence with correct values be desirable?
