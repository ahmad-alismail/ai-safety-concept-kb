# APS Systems (Advanced, Planning, Strategically Aware)

## Definitions
- **[[sources/carlsmith-2021]]**: A system with three properties: (a) **Advanced capability** — outperforms the best humans on tasks that grant significant power (scientific research, business/military/political strategy, engineering, hacking, persuasion/manipulation); (b) **Agentic planning** — makes and executes plans in pursuit of objectives on the basis of world models; (c) **Strategic awareness** — models represent with reasonable accuracy the causal upshot of gaining/maintaining power. — classified as: **compound construct / system type definition**

## Classification
Level of analysis: **compound construct** — a conjunction of three capability properties. Deliberately weaker than "superintelligence" (Bostrom 2014) or "AGI" — Carlsmith argues the risk threshold does not require such strong conditions.

## Relationships
- Composed of: [[concepts/agentic-planning]] + [[concepts/strategic-awareness]] + advanced capabilities
- Subject to: [[concepts/instrumental-convergence]] (when misaligned)
- Distinguished from: "superintelligence," "AGI," "human-level AI" — intentionally sets a lower capability bar
- Related to: [[concepts/situational-awareness]] (strategic awareness is closely related)

## Evidence Summary
- No APS systems exist as of the report. Carlsmith assigns 65% probability that building them becomes feasible by 2070.
- AlphaGo, MuZero, AlphaZero qualify as agentic planners but lack strategic awareness and advanced real-world capabilities.
- GPT-3-like systems may or may not engage in agentic planning internally — this is an open question.

## Open Questions
- Is the APS threshold necessary and sufficient for existential risk, or are there dangerous systems below/above it?
- Can advanced capabilities be achieved via collections of non-APS modular systems, avoiding the risk?
- How should "strategic awareness" be operationalized and measured?
