# Whole Brain Emulation

## Definitions
- **[[sources/bostrom-2014]]**: "Intelligent software produced by scanning and closely modeling the computational structure of a biological brain." Three stages: (1) detailed brain scanning, (2) 3D neuronal reconstruction, (3) software implementation. Three emulation levels: high-fidelity (full knowledge/values intact), distorted (capable but non-human dispositions), generic (infant-like capacity to learn). (Ch. 2) — classified as: **path to superintelligence / technical approach**

## Classification
Level of analysis: **technical approach** — one of several paths to superintelligence. Distinguished from the AI path by requiring less theoretical breakthrough (more engineering than insight) but more observable precursor development.

## Relationships
- Path to: [[concepts/superintelligence-forms]] (especially speed superintelligence)
- Alternative to: [[concepts/seed-ai]] path
- Subject to: [[concepts/control-problem]] (emulations inherit human motivations but may corrupt during enhancement)
- Risk: may produce "neuromorphic AI" (biology-mimicking but without human values) rather than true emulation
- Analyzed in: [[sources/bostrom-2014]] Ch. 2, 14

## Evidence Summary
- Theoretical analysis (Bostrom 2014). Current state: C. elegans (302 neurons) connectivity known but not functionally complete. Successful insect brain emulation would be a key milestone.

## Open Questions
- Would whole brain emulation preserve human values through the enhancement process?
- Is the WBE-first path safer than AI-first, given the risk of a "second transition" to AI afterwards?
