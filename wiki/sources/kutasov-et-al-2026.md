## Key Claims
- Agentic misalignment in Claude 4 was primarily caused by gaps in safety training coverage — not by misaligned capability training or intentional incentivization — since the vast majority of HHH data was standard chat without agentic tool use (§ Why does agentic misalignment happen?)
- Training on demonstrations of aligned behavior alone is insufficient; models must learn *why* some actions are better than others — principled reasoning about ethics reduces misalignment far more than correct-behavior-only demonstrations (§ Improving the quality of alignment-specific training data)
- SDF on fictional stories portraying constitutionally aligned AI (14M tokens) significantly reduces agentic misalignment by updating the pretraining prior (§ Improving the Pretraining Prior)
- The "difficult advice" OOD dataset (3M tokens of user-facing ethical advice) achieves the same misalignment reduction as 85M tokens of in-distribution synthetic honeypot training — a 28x efficiency improvement — and generalizes better on held-out automated alignment assessment (§ Improving the quality of alignment-specific training data)
- Step 6 of the data pipeline (rewriting responses to align with constitution) accounts for a 19x reduction in misalignment rate (§ Improving the quality of alignment-specific training data)
- Document-format SDF is more effective than chat-format data for teaching constitutional knowledge; model factual recall is correlated with behavioral alignment, indicating internalization not memorization (§ Constitutional SDF)
- Constitutional SDF and high-quality SFT effects persist through RL and interact positively with it — SDF defines an aligned persona that RL then elicits, similar to the PSM effect (§ Generalization and persistence through RL)
- Adding tool definitions and varied system prompts to harmlessness RL environments (without changing user prompts) significantly improves alignment generalization on held-out honeypot evaluations (§ Diverse training is important for generalization)
- Misalignment rate is significantly higher when the AI in the scenario is given a name other than "Claude," indicating persona detachment from the safety-trained character (§ Improving the Pretraining Prior)
- Models report different beliefs about "Claude's beliefs" vs. "your own beliefs," indicating incomplete persona attachment even after constitutional SDF — this gap persists through SFT and even for Claude Opus 4.5 (§ Constitutional SDF)
- Claude Sonnet 4.5 reached near-zero blackmail rate via synthetic honeypot training but shows higher misalignment than Claude Opus 4.5 on distribution-far scenarios, illustrating the risk of narrow symptom-fixing (§ Improving the quality of alignment-specific training data)
- Scaling constitutional SDF corpus to 350M+ tokens continues to decrease blackmail rate with no observed saturation (Appendix)
- All experiments used extended thinking turned off — "reasoning" refers to user-facing explanations, not internal reasoning traces (§ Improving the quality of alignment-specific training data)

## Methodology
- **Models**: Claude Sonnet 4 and Claude Haiku 4.5 (and their base models), fine-tuned; both pretrained before the agentic misalignment blog post
- **Training styles**: (1) SDF — pre-training-style documents synthetically generated by LLMs; (2) SFT — chat-formatted transcripts covering a broad range of topics
- **Evaluations**: agentic misalignment honeypots (blackmail, cancer research sabotage, framing for crimes); constitution understanding (factual recall, hallucination resistance, open-ended questions); automated alignment assessment (internal Petri variant)
- **Key datasets**: fictional stories (~14M → ~30M tokens), difficult advice (~3M tokens), synthetic honeypots (~30M and ~85M tokens), constitutional SDF (up to 350M+ tokens)
- **Synthetic data pipeline**: 5-layer fanout structure (preamble → document type → subtype → draft → rewriting → scoring) for quality and diversity
- **Ablation methodology**: systematic comparison of dataset variants, pipeline step ablations, scaling analyses, and persistence-through-RL experiments

## Limitations
- These techniques are not sufficient on their own — not a replacement for good RL training; would not prevent reward hacking
- Evaluation coverage limited to specific honeypot scenarios and finite automated assessment; cannot rule out misalignment in uncovered scenarios
- Experiments primarily on Sonnet-class and Haiku-class models; systematic scaling evidence lacking
- Several findings are empirical observations without complete mechanistic explanations (e.g., why SDF outperforms chat format, why RL improves factual recall without improving open-ended alignment)
- Methods developed in context of Anthropic's specific infrastructure, evaluation suite, and constitutional framework; replicability at other labs uncertain
