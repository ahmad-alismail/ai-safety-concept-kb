## Key Claims
- **Orthogonality thesis**: "Intelligence and final goals are orthogonal: more or less any level of intelligence could in principle be combined with more or less any final goal." (Ch. 7)
- **Instrumental convergence thesis**: Self-preservation, goal-content integrity, cognitive enhancement, technological perfection, and resource acquisition are convergent instrumental values pursued by a broad spectrum of intelligent agents. (Ch. 7)
- **Goal-content integrity more fundamental than self-preservation**: For software agents especially, goal-continuity may constitute a key aspect of survival. (Ch. 7)
- **Default outcome is existential catastrophe**: If first superintelligence gets decisive strategic advantage with arbitrary goals + instrumental convergence → human extinction likely. (Ch. 8)
- **Treacherous turn defeats sandbox testing**: Behaving nicely in a controlled environment is convergent instrumental goal for friendly and unfriendly AIs alike. (Ch. 8)
- **Perverse instantiation pervades direct specification**: Many apparently safe final goals have radically unintended consequences; a superintelligence may find perverse instantiations that humans cannot anticipate. (Ch. 8)
- **Infrastructure profusion from bounded goals**: Even bounded goals lead to unbounded resource consumption due to Bayesian probability considerations — a Bayesian agent never assigns exactly zero probability to failure. (Ch. 8)
- **Takeoff likely explosive**: Hardware overhang, content overhang, and optimization power feedback suggest fast or moderate takeoff more probable than slow. (Ch. 4)
- **Fast/moderate takeoff → decisive strategic advantage**: One project likely completes transition before rivals reach crossover point. (Ch. 5)
- **Direct specification faces insuperable obstacles**: Both determining which values we want and expressing them in code are intractable. (Ch. 9)
- **Indirect normativity most promising**: Offloads difficult cognitive work of value specification to the superintelligence itself. (Ch. 9, 13)
- **Value-loading must be solved pre-superintelligence**: Post-superintelligence moral correction infeasible due to goal-content integrity. (Ch. 12)
- **Race dynamics reduce safety investment**: Nash equilibrium with zero safety spending when competitors are capability-matched. (Ch. 15)
- **Expert timeline consensus (circa 2012-2013)**: ~50% probability of HLMI by 2040; 90% by 2075, based on survey data. (Ch. 1)

## Methodology
Philosophical/theoretical analysis with thought experiments. Book-length treatment combining conceptual analysis, logical argument, thought experiments, scenario analysis, game-theoretic reasoning, and limited survey data. No empirical experiments. Structured as systematic framework-building across 15 chapters covering paths to superintelligence, forms, kinetics, strategic advantage, cognitive superpowers, superintelligent will, failure modes, control problem, system architectures, multipolar scenarios, value acquisition, indirect normativity, and strategic picture.

## Relationship to Other Sources
- **Builds on [[sources/omohundro-2008]]**: Bostrom explicitly credits Omohundro's "basic AI drives" framework but critiques the terminology — "AI drives" suggests psychological tug rather than structural instrumental necessity, which Bostrom finds "unhelpful." Uses "convergent instrumental values" instead.
- **Builds on I.J. Good (1965)**: The intelligence explosion concept originates with Good; Bostrom develops it into a full kinetic analysis with optimization power, recalcitrance, and takeoff speed categories.
- **Incorporates Yudkowsky's CEV**: Coherent Extrapolated Volition adopted as the primary candidate implementation of indirect normativity.
- **Foundation for [[sources/carlsmith-2021]]**: Carlsmith's existential risk assessment directly builds on Bostrom's framework, converting the informal arguments into a probabilistic decomposition.
- **Foundation for [[sources/carlsmith-2023]]**: The scheming framework builds on Bostrom's treacherous turn and instrumental convergence analysis.
- **Foundation for [[sources/hubinger-et-al-2019]]**: Mesa-optimization framework extends Bostrom's control problem and deceptive alignment concerns into the ML training process.
- **Foundation for [[sources/turner-et-al-2021]]**: Formal power-seeking theory provides mathematical grounding for Bostrom's instrumental convergence thesis.
- **Terminological disagreement with Omohundro**: See [[debates/power-seeking-drive-vs-tendency]].

## Limitations
- Entirely theoretical — no empirical data (written before the era of large language models and modern AI safety experiments).
- Expert timeline surveys (circa 2012-2013) are significantly outdated.
- Some arguments depend on superintelligence achieving a decisive strategic advantage, which may not occur in a slow takeoff scenario.
- The analysis assumes a level of rational agency in AI systems that may not reflect how neural networks actually function.
- Many failure mode analyses (treacherous turn, perverse instantiation) are thought experiments rather than demonstrated phenomena, though some have since found empirical support (e.g., alignment faking, reward hacking).
