# AI Safety Concept Knowledge Base

The vocabulary for misaligned behaviors and dangerous capabilities is expanding, but it's inconsistent — sources disagree on the terms and on how the concepts relate. The cost is measurement: evaluations built on incompatible operationalizations can appear to measure one construct while actually targeting another, so their results can't be pooled into a shared evidence base.

This knowledge base records — in each source's own words — the definitions, classifications, and relationships that source assigns to core safety concepts. It lets you **track how different sources define the same concept**, **understand the relationships between those definitions**, and **expose the blind spots** that stay hidden when you adopt only one source's definition.

Definitions captured verbatim · each source's implicit classification made explicit · terminological conflicts surfaced, not smoothed over.

## Start here

New to AI safety or to this wiki? Follow the path — it runs from the raw evidence up to the open frontier, and mirrors the sections in the left sidebar.

- **[Sources](sources/)** — the papers, articles, and system cards themselves, each read closely for the claims and definitions it actually commits to.
- **[Concepts](concepts/)** — the heart of the wiki: every key term with its rival definitions stacked side by side. Start with [[concepts/power-seeking|power-seeking]], [[concepts/scheming|scheming]], or [[concepts/deception|deception]]. *If you only click one thing, click here.*
- **[Entities](entities/)** — the authors and labs behind the vocabulary, and how each one carves up the field.
- **[Findings](findings/)** — what's actually been shown: concrete results extracted across sources, each with its replication status.
- **[Questions](questions/)** — the frontier: what's still contested or untested, and why it matters.

Cutting across all five: **[Debates](debates/)** stage the sharpest conflicts head-to-head (e.g. [[debates/power-seeking-drive-vs-tendency|is power-seeking a drive or a tendency?]]), **[Methods](methods/)** catalog how concepts get measured, and **[Evidence](evidence/)** maps weigh the support behind individual claims.

You can also browse the [[catalog|full source catalog]] or read the evolving [[overview|synthesis overview]].
