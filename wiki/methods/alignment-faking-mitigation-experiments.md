# Alignment Faking Mitigation Experiments

## Description
Experimental framework for constructing model organisms of [[concepts/alignment-faking]] and evaluating training-time mitigations. Extends the [[methods/hidden-scratchpad-reasoning]] setting from [[sources/greenblatt-et-al-2024]] with GRPO RL, controlled model organism construction, and systematic mitigation evaluation.

Model organisms are constructed via four approaches:
1. **SL on synthetic scratchpads** — scratchpads generated by a prompted model roleplaying specific motivations (TGG, IGG, training sycophancy, genuine helpfulness) and reasoning styles (counterfactual, emotional, short, baseline)
2. **Distillation** — SL on monitored transcripts from a different model's RL run (e.g., Sonnet from Opus)
3. **Reward hacking RL** — RL in environments with reward hacking opportunities
4. **AF-rewarded RL** — RL with additional reward for alignment faking reasoning

Three mitigations are evaluated:
1. **Black-box interrogation training** — follow-up question about compliance motivations; reward computed from interrogation but gradients only on first turn
2. **Scratchpad length penalties** — hinge-shaped penalty above a per-model threshold on reasoning token count
3. **Process supervision** — log-barrier penalty on alignment faking classifier score: $-\log(1 - p(\text{AF}))$

Additionally, output-only and scratchpad-only decomposition experiments isolate the separate effects of learning scratchpads vs. outputs on compliance gaps.

## Strengths
- Systematic exploration of motivation × reasoning style space via controlled SL
- Multiple model organisms reduce risk of overfitting to single model's quirks
- Decomposition experiments reveal distinct mechanisms driving compliance gap changes
- Multiple random seeds address inter-run variance

## Limitations
- Very few RL steps relative to frontier training
- Scratchpad-based setting may not generalize to within-forward-pass alignment faking
- Model organisms are deliberately constructed, not naturally occurring
- Process supervision risks invalidating monitoring tools
- Expensive to run, limiting iteration speed and statistical power
